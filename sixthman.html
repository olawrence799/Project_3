Sixth Man of the Year Stats Summary

The assist percentage for the Sixth Man of they Year has gradually declined over the past decade to be at around the same percentage as the league average. Over time the Sixth man of the Year has become more directly involved in the game, relying less on his teammates for scoring and making more field goal attempts. Win Share is decreasing which could possibly be because good players don’t sit on the bench anymore (smaller supply of star players). It could also be because the Sixth Man of the Year is more focused on improving their own abilities and being in the spotlight instead of leveraging the skills of more experienced teammates (Higher 3PA & 2PA with FG% similar to the league average has lowered their WS and AST% over time). The sixth man of the year is also becoming gradually better at keeping the ball in their possession (TOV% decreasing) which could be a reason why they get more play time (MP) than the league average. The True Salary of the Sixth Man of the Year has gradually dropped over the past decade to a value similar to the league average possibly meaning that these players aren’t truly valuable to the team or its owners but are still widely recognized/ appreciated by fans because of above average attempts to score. It seems that the Sixth man of the Year is becoming increasingly difficult to predict and the award is becoming less prestigious because the players are making less of a contribution to their team over time with a decreasing value over replacement player (VORP).
