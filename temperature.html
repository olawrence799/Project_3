Latitude vs Max Temperature (F)

The figure above plots the observed maximum temperature (y-axis) against the city latitude (x-axis). This scatter plot reveals a fairly clear relationship between temperature and latitude: temperature tends to be highest at the equator (latitude=0). Interestingly, the northern hemisphere appears to have colder temperatures than the southern hemisphere at comparable distances from the equator. Further, there is more temperature variance across cities with latitude > 0, shown by the vertical spread in data points.
